Joyce
https://ajoycel.github.io/cart263/project2/

I really enjoyed Joyce’s project since it was quite different from the other projects I saw in the class. Again this project, being the continuation of her first project, was not an actual game,but a journal. I enjoy all the extra features she added such as the different fonts and the option to have different folders. I like how she included 3D into her project. Although the main journaling experience is in 2D, when the user clicks into the game, there is a 3D house that the user has to click on to access the journal. I think this is a very creative way to start the experience as it makes the user “go home” to access the journal since most people journal in the privacy of their own home. 

I enjoy the addition of details in each of her backgrounds. Joyce added a few birds, flowers that change colors every time the user clicks on them and the changes in size in the Iilypads. My favourite is still the pond setting. The fishes change colors and directions when the user clicks on them, which adds a touch of interactivity outside of the main activity. I enjoyed the sound of moving water and birds that Joyce added. It reminds me of writing in my journal by the river years ago, making the experience of writing more immersive. 

The code was also very organized. There were multiple files and there was a lot of space between each section of code, making it easy to navigate. It did not feel too cluttered or overwhelming, and I could easily recognize the elements we learned in class. 

Overall, my experience with this project was very enjoyable. It balanced aesthetic tranquility with functional design. By offering customizable environments, it transformed a simple text-entry task into an immersive digital space with narrative storytelling.


Jason and Haolei
https://lee-sifu.github.io/Project-2_Cart263/Project-2_Cart263-main

Jason and Haolei’s project is unlike any other project I have seen so far. The idea is simply to click on the cube and the background changes colors and images until the system overloads and it resets. However, the whole experience makes it so much more entertaining and fun. 

I was so stimulated from all the colors and texts that I could not look away from my screen. The tension builds up as the user clicks the square. The game starts off as a cube in the middle of the screen and there is confetti when the user clicks on the cube. With every click, there are more colors, more images and more words until the game overloads and resets by itself. Everything looked very clean, polished and very professional.

The code was also very organized. By seeing the project, I thought the code would be very complicated and crowded, but it was not. It was clean and had plenty of space in between each function. It was easy to navigate and recognize the notions we saw in class. 

Overall, the game’s simple mechanics did not take away from the fun. This game was stimulating, colorful and very professional, showing that games do not need to have complicated rules to be fun. 


Shrook
https://shrook12.github.io/cart263/project2-cart263/

Shrook’s project was also very eyecatching. There is a spaceship and a few objects floating in the void of space. When the player clicks on the up and down button, the spaceship moves through the space as if it was going flying towards or away from the objects. 

I like the lighting in her environment. The spaceship’s metallic material reflected the warm light which made it look really realistic. It also made the whole environment less boring with a few patches of orange-yellow which reminds me of the sun. I also love the shift in perspective when the spaceship passes the objects in the middle. It felt like I was the pilot who turned around to look behind me as I passed a checkpoint in space. It makes the experience so much more immersive and captivating. I loved trying out the different angles and perspectives this environment offered. 

Her code, however, was a bit crowded and I found myself reading a few lines twice in a row. However, it was easy to recognize the notions we learned in class and see her thought process through her code. 

Overall, Shrook’s project shows wonderful worldbuilding using 3D by strategically placing lights and objects around the space with shifts in perspectives, making the project feel immersive. Although the code was slightly more crowded than the other projects I looked at, it still shows that she understands class material.
