Joyce
https://ajoycel.github.io/cart263/project1/

I really enjoyed Joyce’s project since it was quite different from the other projects I saw in the class. It was not an actual game, just a journal to write in. As someone who enjoys journaling, I had a lot of fun just writing in it and reflecting on my own thoughts.

I liked that there were a few different backgrounds to choose from. I thought that idea was very unique since it allowed for some customization. I especially like the pond setting for the background. The fished and lilypads moving in the water made the overall visuals more dynamic and  interesting to look at. I had fun just looking at the fish swimming in the pond while I was writing in the journal. The code was also very well organized. 

There were multiple files and there was a lot of space between each section of code, making it easy to navigate. It did not feel too cluttered or overwhelming, and I could easily recognize the elements we learned in class. 

Overall, my experience with this project was very enjoyable. It balanced aesthetic tranquility with functional design. By offering customizable environments, it transformed a simple text-entry task into an immersive digital space.


Jay, Yann and Zhuoheng
https://sleepyrayray.github.io/project-1/

This project immediately caught my attention because of its interesting sound effects. Being a Pacman inspired game, the mechanics were intuitive and easy to understand right away. Also, being a multiplayer game made it stand out from the rest of the game projects in the class. It was fun to play a nostalgic game with someone else.

What I  enjoyed the most about this project were the sound effects. They were incredibly funny and added a layer of personality and humor that made the gameplay much more engaging. The alarm and the cow sound effect were my favourites and clearly indicated the start and end of the game in a unique way. 

The code was also well organized, making everything easy to find. Everything was separated into multiple JavaScript files with each element having its own file. 

Overall, this game felt nostalgic, funny and organized making for a memorable multiplayer experience that successfully balanced classic arcade mechanics with a unique, playful personality


Norah and Leah
https://norahwilcox.github.io/CART263/projects/project-1/Project1/

Norah and Leah’s project stood out because of its visuals. It is similar to a photo booth with multiple filters to take interesting photos with friends. The background was a slightly blurred old Windows interface, which added a nice touch of digital nostalgia. I liked how easy it was to navigate. It had a simplistic design and it was very easy to switch between filters, which kept the experience fast-paced and encouraged me to experiment with all the different looks. The inclusion of the countdown timer and the gallery view at the end made it feel like a complete, functional application.

The code was long but organized. There is a lot of space between each section, making it easy to read and understand. The comments on top of each section also helped with identifying the purpose of each section of code. 

Overall, this combination of thoughtful documentation and a playful retro aesthetic made the project feel  very polished and professional.
